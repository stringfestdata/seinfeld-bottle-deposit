Context

Why Michigan? Michigan's Bottle Bill (1976) returns 10 cents per can -- the highest in the US and double New York's 5 cent deposit. Every can you haul there is worth a dime. Sounds easy until you pay for gas.

Newman's secret edge: His USPS mail route already ran to Saginaw. The government paid his gas. His break-even was literally zero cans, which is why Kramer called it "practically free money." He was right, but only about Newman's situation -- not a normal person's.

Jon Voight's* LeBaron: Appears in Season 3, Episode 2. It's a wood-paneled Town and Country-style convertible -- one of the more outrageous props in the show's history. George buys it convinced celebrity provenance adds value. A bite mark later complicates things. (*Voight, not Voight.)

Newman's mail truck: The big USPS step-van, not the small LLV. Tall, boxy, underpowered, and fueled entirely by government contracts and spite. Newman drove it across state lines stuffed with garbage bags full of cans.
