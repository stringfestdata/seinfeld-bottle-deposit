📺 Context

Why Michigan? Michigan's Bottle Bill (1976) returns 10¢ per can — the highest in the US. New York pays 5¢. Net gain per can: 5¢. Sounds easy. Then you pay for gas.

Newman's secret edge: His USPS route already ran to Saginaw. The government paid his gas. His break-even was literally 0 cans — which is why Kramer called it "practically free money." He was right, but only about Newman's situation.

Jon Voight's* LeBaron: S3 E2. Kramer buys it convinced celebrity provenance adds value. A bite mark later complicates things. (*Voight, not Voight.)

The USPS LLV: Grumman-built Long Life Vehicle, aluminum body, ~10 MPG, right-hand drive. Built to last 24 years. Newman's ran entirely on resentment.
